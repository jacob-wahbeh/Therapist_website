About & My Approach

Combining evidence-based psychotherapy with faith-informed wisdom for authentic healing

ISTDP: Intensive Short-Term Dynamic Psychotherapy

ISTDP is a powerful, evidence-based form of psychotherapy that focuses on helping you access and experience emotions that have been buried or avoided, often since childhood. Unlike traditional talk therapy that can take years, ISTDP is designed to create meaningful change in a shorter timeframe.

How ISTDP Works

At its core, ISTDP recognizes that many psychological symptoms, such as anxiety, depression, and chronic relationship problems, are often the result of unconscious defenses against painful emotions. By gently but directly addressing these defenses, we can access the underlying feelings and resolve long-standing patterns.

What Makes ISTDP Different

  • Focuses on emotional experiencing, not just intellectual understanding
  • Actively works to break through psychological defenses
  • Creates rapid, lasting change through deep emotional processing
  • Extensively researched with strong evidence for effectiveness
  • Addresses root causes rather than just managing symptoms

Conditions ISTDP Can Help

  • Anxiety disorders and panic attacks
  • Depression and persistent sadness
  • Relationship difficulties and attachment issues
  • Psychosomatic symptoms (physical symptoms with emotional origins)
  • Self-defeating patterns and self-sabotage
  • Grief and loss
  • Trauma and PTSD

Faith-Integrated Psychotherapy

An Orthodox Christian Approach to Mental Health

For clients who desire it, I offer therapy that thoughtfully integrates Orthodox Christian spirituality with evidence-based psychological treatment. This approach recognizes that for many people, faith is not separate from mental health. It is central to understanding who we are and how we heal.

The Orthodox Christian Lens

Orthodox Christianity has a rich tradition of understanding the human person, including the relationship between body, soul, and spirit, the nature of passions and virtues, and the path toward theosis (union with God). This ancient wisdom offers profound insights that complement modern psychological understanding.

What Faith-Integrated Therapy Looks Like

  • Respecting your faith as a source of strength and meaning
  • Integrating spiritual practices when appropriate and desired
  • Exploring how faith intersects with psychological struggles
  • Addressing spiritual challenges alongside emotional ones
  • Drawing on the wisdom of the Church Fathers and Orthodox tradition
  • Maintaining proper boundaries between therapy and spiritual direction

For Everyone

While I offer faith-integrated therapy for those who want it, my practice warmly welcomes people of all faiths and no faith. The core principles of compassionate, effective therapy remain the same regardless of spiritual orientation.

My Therapeutic Philosophy

I believe that every person has an innate capacity for healing and growth. My role is not to fix you, because you are not broken. Rather, it is to create conditions where you can access your own wisdom, feel what you have been avoiding, and make the changes you have always known you needed to make.

Real therapy is not always comfortable. It requires honesty, courage, and a willingness to face difficult truths. But if you are ready to do that work, the rewards (greater peace, deeper relationships, authentic self-expression) are profound.
